Reduction of Nitrate Levels in Water Sources

During my job attachment, I was a part of an experimental project that researched on reducing the amount of nitrate levels in water using copper, nickel and carbon fibre paper via electrochemical reduction. This project was performed during my 2 months spent in the NUS laboratory. We were first tasked to research an issue that was prevalent in the world right now. We found out that having high levels of nitrate in water sources is becoming a growing problem due to excessive usage of nitrogen fertilisers and discharge of untreated wastewater. It has been found that excessive nitrate levels in water can lead to environmental problems as well cause unwanted body harm if consumed. Methods to reduce this issue typically use precious metal which makes the method more expensive. Thus we decided to research on a method to help reduce this cost, mainly by using more cost-friendly and widely available material such as copper. We were tasked to submit documentation on our research, intermittent project progress levels as well as a final lab report to conclude our project. This was one of the bigger scale projects I have had the opportunity to partake in and it has definitely helped me in my project management and time management skills.

Community Projects

Project CO2

3 of my friends and I had decided to set up a values-in-action (VIA) project to help raise awareness about climate change due to the increasing levels of carbon dioxide in the air. This campaign allowed students from our school to participate in challenges to accumulate VIA points for themselves. Being part of the executive committee, I helped the team in planning the project as well as drafting out the proposal to submit to our teacher in charge. After the project had been officially launched, I helped in managing the social media account as well as validating the submissions of our participants. I continuously communicated with the participants about any changes as well as answered any queries they had about the project. If any participant had issues with their submissions, I would also help to communicate with them so that they could resubmit the challenge. This entire project helped me strengthen my leadership skills as well as project management skills. This was the first big-scale project I had helped manage and it was a very fun and meaningful experience.

It is only in the more recent years that I have assumed a higher role of being part of the overall-in-charge team of the camp. One of my more recent roles was being the admin leader of the camp. I was also in-charge of safekeeping all of their valuables such as their phones and wallets. Other tasks included contacting the participants' parents and taking note of dietary requirements. These positions I have had the opportunity of taking up in church have allowed me to really step up in terms of leadership as well as project management. I also had to learn how to manage my tasks from church with my school work and CCA which has taught me a lot more about good time management.
